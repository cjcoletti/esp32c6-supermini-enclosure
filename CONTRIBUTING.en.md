# Contributing
[![Português](https://img.shields.io/badge/Language-Português-inactive)](CONTRIBUTING.md)[![English](https://img.shields.io/badge/_English-blue)](CONTRIBUTING.en.md)

Thank you for your interest in **ESP32-C6 Super Mini Enclosure**!

This project is maintained personally, as a contribution to the Matter/ESP32 community. Suggestions and ideas are very welcome — they help me think about fit improvements, internal layout, and use cases I may not have considered.

## How to participate

### Suggestions and ideas

Open an [Issue](https://github.com/cjcoletti/esp32c6-supermini-enclosure/issues/new/choose) using the **Ideia ou sugestão** template. Describe:

- the context or problem you identified (fit, openings, printability, etc.);
- your proposal in text (how you imagine it would work);
- hardware and environment, if relevant (board, PSU, printer, filament).

No code is required — I prefer to understand the idea and implement it at my own pace, if it makes sense for the project.

### Reporting bugs

If something did not work as expected (tight fit, misaligned USB-C, incorrect hole, etc.), use the **Reportar bug** template. Include steps to reproduce, print environment details, and photos if possible.

### Discussions

You can also use [this space](https://github.com/cjcoletti/esp32c6-supermini-enclosure/discussions) to share ideas or ask questions more openly.

## What I do not accept

**Pull Requests** — please do not send PRs with CAD/STL file changes. I prefer to implement changes myself, at my own pace.

Exception: if you find a clear typo in the documentation (`README`, etc.), you may open an Issue pointing to the passage; I will evaluate case by case.

## Forks

You are free to **fork** and adapt the project to your needs, under the [MIT license](LICENSE). If you publish an interesting variant, feel free to mention it in an Issue or Discussion — I am happy to hear how others are using the project.

## Expectations

- There is no official support, response SLA, or guarantee of implementation.
- Not every suggestion will be adopted — and that is fine. I appreciate the time people take to share ideas.
- I respond when I can, within the time this personal project allows.
